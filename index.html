Trip notes

🐻Bears: Shenandoah NP requires proper food storage. Store all food in bear-resistant containers or in your vehicle overnight — never leave food unattended at camp.
🦟Ticks: Very common in Shenandoah in June. Apply repellent daily and do a full-body check each evening. Carry a tick removal tool on every hike.
🪵Firewood: Purchase at the campground — do not bring wood from home due to invasive species risk.
📞Cabin linens: If using Scenario B or C, call Lewis Mountain to confirm what bedding the cabin includes before packing.
